For 2021 I've decided to resume my reading habits and set a target of reading an average of 1 book per month, investing in reviewing and consolidate past learnings and also add new skills and perspectives.

Here are the ones I've read until now and fully recommend to everyone on SW Engineering, either on individual contributor either on a management path:

- (Jan'2021) [The Phoenix project](https://www.amazon.com/Phoenix-Project-DevOps-Helping-Business/dp/1942788290/ref=sr_1_1?dchild=1&keywords=phoenix+project+gene+kim&qid=1616867745&sr=8-1) - by Gene Kim, A Novel About IT, DevOps, and Helping Your Business Win
- (Jan'2021) [The Radical Candor](https://www.amazon.com/Radical-Candor-KIM-SCOTT/dp/1509845380/ref=sr_1_5?crid=136I24WXPARD4&dchild=1&keywords=radical+candor+by+kim+scott&qid=1616868049&s=books&sprefix=radical+candor+%2Caps%2C498&sr=1-5) - by Kim Scott, How to Get What You Want by Saying What You Mean
- (Fev'2021) [The Unicorn Project](https://www.amazon.com/Unicorn-Project-Developers-Disruption-Thriving/dp/1942788762/ref=sr_1_3?dchild=1&keywords=phoenix+project+gene+kim&qid=1616867745&sr=8-3) - by Gene Kim, A Novel about Developers, Digital Disruption and Thriving in the Age of Data
- (Fev'2021) [Essential Scrum](https://www.amazon.com/Essential-Scrum-Practical-Addison-Wesley-Signature/dp/0137043295/ref=sr_1_1?dchild=1&keywords=scrum+essentials&qid=1616867983&sr=8-1) - by Kenneth Rubin, A Practical Guide to the Most Popular Agile Process
- (Mar'2021) [The Manager's Path](https://www.amazon.com/Managers-Path-Leaders-Navigating-Growth/dp/1491973897/ref=sr_1_1?dchild=1&keywords=managers+path&qid=1616868019&sr=8-1) - by Camille Fournier, A Guide for Tech Leaders Navigating Growth and Change
